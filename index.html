how we worked this out

The numbers on this page come from Elexon, the official body that settles Britain's electricity market, via its free public data service. When the grid can't carry the wind, the system operator accepts paid "turn-down" offers from wind farms and buys replacement energy from other generators — we add up both costs, half-hour by half-hour, counting only turn-downs flagged as grid-constraint actions. Settled days update each morning; today's figure updates through the day, and the page re-checks the feed every 15 minutes. The "a second" rates shown are the recent average pace, for scale — the headline figures themselves are ledger values, not extrapolations. If the live feed is unavailable, the page falls back to an even-rate estimate based on ~£1.5bn a year (£47.56 a second). The cost is paid through energy bills, not by the taxpayer.
